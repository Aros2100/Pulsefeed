Abstract
BackgroundRuptured vertebral artery dissecting aneurysms (VADAs) present significant surgical challenges due to their complex anatomy and the need for hyperacute intervention. The optimal surgical strategy remains controversial.
MethodsWe retrospectively reviewed 23 consecutive patients with ruptured VADAs treated at our institution between 2015 and 2023. All patients underwent surgical intervention within 72 hours of initial hemorrhage. Perforator bypass was performed when the posterior inferior cerebellar artery territory was at risk.
ResultsGood neurological outcomes (mRS 0–2) were achieved in 78% of patients at 6-month follow-up. Perforator bypass was successfully performed in 8 patients with no bypass-related complications.
ConclusionsHyperacute surgical intervention with perforator bypass is feasible and safe for ruptured VADAs, providing excellent outcomes in selected patients.
