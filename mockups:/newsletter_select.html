Optimizing surgical strategies for ruptured vertebral artery dissecting aneurysms: experience with perforator bypass and hyperacute management
18 Feb 2026 · Ota N et al. · PMID 41706223 ↗
AI Summary
This retrospective study demonstrates that hyperacute surgical intervention with perforator bypass is both feasible and safe for ruptured vertebral artery dissecting aneurysms, achieving good neurological outcomes (mRS 0–2) in 78% of patients at 6-month follow-up.

Abstract
BackgroundRuptured vertebral artery dissecting aneurysms present significant surgical challenges due to complex anatomy and need for hyperacute intervention. The optimal surgical strategy remains controversial.
MethodsWe retrospectively reviewed 23 consecutive patients treated between 2015 and 2023. All patients underwent surgical intervention within 72 hours of initial hemorrhage.
ResultsGood neurological outcomes (mRS 0–2) were achieved in 78% of patients at 6-month follow-up. Perforator bypass was successfully performed in 8 patients with no complications.
ConclusionsHyperacute surgical intervention with perforator bypass is feasible and safe, providing excellent outcomes in selected patients.
